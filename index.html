Pizza Margherita

Probably the most famous topping in the world, margherita was originally created by Raffaele Esposito when the Queen Margherita di Savoia (hence why the name) asked for a pizza.
This is a simple pizza, made of tomatoes sauce, parmesan/pecorino mix, fior di latte, basil & olive oil.

Pizza Marinara

One of the oldest pizzas known, usually choose by the lowest ranks of society, since it had a very few and low cost ingredients. Simply: tomatoes sauce, extra virgin olive oil, and oregano. Only after hearing people complaining of being bland, a pizzaiolo working near the port of Naples decided to add garlic and basil.

Pizza Capricciosa

This was known in the common pizzaiolo dialet as the leftovers pizza, for which they used all the leftover ingredients of the night. And because of that, it used to change every night due to different ingredients that were leftover. It has now a very specific selection of ingredients, which are tomatoes sauce, mozzarella, cooked ham, mushrooms, black olives and artichokes hearts.

Pizza & Dintorni

This pizza was my own creation, hence why the Pizza&Dintorni name. This is a vegetarian pizza made with tomatoes sauce, mozzarella, fried aubergine (cut lenghtwise), parmesan crisps and plenty of basil.

Pizza Cosacca

Pizza Cosacca is a fusion between Margherita and Marinara, where instead of mozzarella the pizzaiolo uses freshly grated parmesan. History tells that this topping was born in the 1800, when the Zar Nicola I visited Naples. But we are able to learn that this was actually born earlier and it was the most common pizza that pizzaiolos used to make for themselves at the end of the shift, when basil and mozzarella were finished and there was only parmesan available.

Direct Method

Recipe

  • 1Kg Strong Flour
  • 680ml cold water
  • 2grs of fresh yeast
  • 25gr salt

Mix yeast in the water well, and start adding half of the water to the flour, mixing by hand. Then slowly add the rest of the water and salt mix and knead for 20 mins. Rest in the fridge for 18hrs. Balling up process, and in fridge until cooking time (up to 6hrs).
